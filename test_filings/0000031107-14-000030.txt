<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">Naugatuck, CT</font> &#8211; The Eastern Company (NASDAQ-EML) today announced that it has completed the acquisition of the Assets and Business of Argo Transdata located in Clinton, Connecticut.&#160;&#160;Argo is a manufacturer and supplier of printed circuit board assemblies used in medical devices, measurement and control equipment and in many other industrial products and has been operating successfully since 1969.&#160;&#160;The terms of the transaction were not disclosed. The acquisition will increase sales by approximately 4% and will be accretive to Eastern earnings.</font></div>

<div style="TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; FONT-SIZE: 10pt">Mr. Leonard F. Leganza, Chairman, President and CEO stated: &#8220;The acquisition of Argo by Eastern, as have several other acquisitions, will also be an excellent addition for The Eastern Company. Argo&#8217;s business, like that of Eastern, is as a manufacturer of components that, in most cases, are found and used in products made by Original Equipment Manufacturers. In addition, this business represents a new initiative and further diversification of Eastern&#8217;s markets and security segments. Its operations will become part of our Security Products Group which utilizes printed circuit boards in several of its products.&#160;We plan to expand this business in the coming years both organically and through acquisitions of similar and technologically related companies.&#8221;</font></div>
